Review the strategy before we dial. You can barge in or end the call at any time.

Leverage points (ranked)

  • 1

    Competitor pricing

    "T-Mobile Home Internet is offering $50/mo for the same 400 Mbps tier you're paying $94 for." Agent cites exact price.

  • 2

    3-year tenure

    "I have been a customer for 3 years on autopay." Loyalty headroom ~$10/mo additional discount.

  • 3

    No-bundle status

    Internet-only account. Agent will threaten to add a competitor phone plan and drop Xfinity entirely.

  • —

    No recent rate hike on file

    This leverage point is unavailable — your bill history doesn't show a rate increase. Agent will skip this.

Opening script

[Agent opens. Legal disclosure first.]
AGENT: "Hi, this is an AI voice assistant calling on behalf of the account holder. This call is being recorded for quality purposes. Is it okay to proceed?"

REP: "Sure, go ahead."

AGENT: "Hi — I've been reviewing my account and the cost has gotten a bit high for my budget. I've been looking at T-Mobile Home Internet which is offering $50 a month for a similar 400 Mbps tier. Before I make any changes, I wanted to see what Xfinity can do to keep me as a customer."

[Wait for response. Never accept first offer.]
Escalation ladder: Frontline rep says no twice → ask for Loyalty/Retention → offer under 15% → ask for supervisor → under $10 off → mention FCC informal complaint.
